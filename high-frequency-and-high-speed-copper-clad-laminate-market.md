Metal substrates use copper, aluminum, or iron as the base material, providing excellent thermal conductivity. Ceramic substrates are made of ceramic materials like aluminum oxide or aluminum nitride, offering high thermal conductivity, electrical insulation, and stability. These materials are crucial in high-frequency and high-speed electronic applications.</p></p>
