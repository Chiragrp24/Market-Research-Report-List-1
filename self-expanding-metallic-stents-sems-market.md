<p><p>Self-Expanding Metallic Stents (SEMS) are medical devices used in minimally invasive procedures to treat narrow or blocked blood vessels. They are available in two market types - stainless steel and Nitinol. Stainless steel stents are made from a corrosion-resistant alloy, offering excellent radial strength and wide availability. Nitinol stents are made from a shape memory alloy that exhibits superelastic properties, allowing them to self-expand when deployed. Nitinol stents offer better conformability and flexibility compared to stainless steel stents, providing an improved patient experience during stenting procedures.</p></p>
