<p><p>The in-counter barcode scanners market includes both one-dimensional (1D) and two-dimensional (2D) barcode scanners. One-dimensional barcode scanners are the traditional type that can read linear barcodes, which are a series of parallel lines. They are widely used in retail environments due to their reliability and low cost. On the other hand, two-dimensional barcode scanners can read both linear and matrix barcodes, which consist of squares or dots arranged in a pattern. These scanners are more versatile as they can store more data, including text, images, and website links.</p></p>
