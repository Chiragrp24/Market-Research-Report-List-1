<p><p>The electroactive polymer market includes various types such as conductive plastics, inherently conductive polymers (ICPs), and inherently dissipative polymers (IDPs). Conductive plastics are traditional polymers infused with conductive additives to enhance their electrical conductivity. Inherently conductive polymers have conductive properties by nature without the need for additives. Inherently dissipative polymers, on the other hand, are designed to dissipate static charge from surfaces, making them suitable for applications where static electricity buildup needs to be avoided. These different types cater to diverse needs in the electrical and electronics industry.</p></p>
