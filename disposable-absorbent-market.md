<p><p>The disposable absorbent market encompasses three main types: natural adsorption, synthetic adsorption, and others. Natural adsorption refers to absorbents made from natural materials like cotton, cellulose, or wood pulp that can absorb liquids through their natural capillary action. Synthetic adsorption involves absorbents created from synthetic materials such as superabsorbent polymers or hydrogels, which can absorb liquid multiple times their weight. The "others" category includes alternative absorbent materials, such as bio-based or nanotechnology-based absorbents, that offer unique properties or environmental advantages compared to traditional absorbents.</p></p>
