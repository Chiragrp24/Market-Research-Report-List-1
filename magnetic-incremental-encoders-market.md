<p><p>Magnetic incremental encoders are devices used to measure the position and movement of objects. The linear magnetic encoder is designed to measure linear motion, such as the position of a machine tool. It utilizes a magnetic strip and a sensor to accurately determine the object's displacement. On the other hand, rotary magnetic encoders are used to measure rotational motion, such as the angle of a robotic arm. These encoders use a magnet and a sensor to detect the rotation and provide precise position information. Both types of encoders are vital in various industries for precise motion control and monitoring.</p></p>
