<p><p>Polyvinyl alcohol (PVOH), commonly known as Poval, is a water-soluble synthetic polymer. It is produced from the polymerization of vinyl acetate and then hydrolyzed to form PVOH. Poval has several important properties, including high tensile strength, excellent film-forming ability, good barrier properties, and resistance to oils, solvents, and chemicals. Due to these properties, Poval finds extensive applications in various industries, including textiles, paper, adhesives, packaging, electronics, and construction.</p><p>The Poval market is experiencing significant growth and is expected to expand at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.8% during the forecast period. The increasing demand for Poval in various end-use industries is primarily driving this market growth. </p><p>In the textiles industry, Poval is commonly used as a sizing agent, providing smoothness and strength to the fibers. The growing demand for PVA fibers in the textile industry is fueling the demand for Poval. Additionally, the increasing consumption of Poval in the packaging industry is driving market growth. Poval films and coatings are widely used for food packaging due to their excellent barrier properties, moisture resistance, and biodegradability.</p><p>Furthermore, the construction industry is also a significant consumer of Poval. It is used in cement-based products, such as mortar and grouts, as a binder and a key ingredient for improving adhesion, workability, and durability. The construction industry's growth, specifically in emerging economies, is propelling the demand for Poval.</p><p>In terms of regional analysis, Asia Pacific is expected to dominate the Poval market during the forecast period.
