Low intensity lights are typically used for structures that are 150 feet or shorter, such as communication towers. Medium intensity lights are suitable for structures between 200 and 500 feet, like wind turbines. High intensity lights are employed in structures taller than 500 feet, such as high-rise buildings and tall towers. These lights ensure visibility and safety by facilitating the identification of structures during nighttime or low visibility conditions.</p></p>
