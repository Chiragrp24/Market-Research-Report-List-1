<p><p>The glaze and icing stabilizers market includes two types of additives: buffering agents and emulsifiers. Buffering agents are substances that help maintain the pH balance of glazes and icings, preventing them from becoming too acidic or alkaline. They stabilize the product's texture and improve its shelf life. Emulsifiers, on the other hand, are additives that help ingredients blend together smoothly and prevent separation. They improve the stability of the glaze or icing, ensuring a consistent and appealing appearance. Both types of stabilizers play a crucial role in enhancing the quality of glazes and icings.</p></p>
