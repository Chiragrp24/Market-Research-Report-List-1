The pharmaceutical industry uses it as a reagent in the synthesis of various pharmaceutical compounds. The electronics industry also utilizes anhydrous hydrogen chloride for cleaning and etching electronic components.</p><p>Growing population, urbanization, and industrialization in developing economies are driving the demand for chemicals and pharmaceuticals, thereby fueling the growth of the anhydrous hydrogen chloride market.
