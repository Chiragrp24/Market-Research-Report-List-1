<p><p>The automotive ultracapacitor market consists of two main types: electrostatic double-layer capacitors (EDLCs) and hybrid capacitors. EDLCs rely on the separation of charge in an electric field and can store a large amount of energy. Hybrid capacitors combine the energy storage capabilities of EDLCs with the high energy density of batteries. Both types of ultracapacitors are used in automotive applications to provide quick bursts of power, energy storage, and support to the vehicle's electrical systems.</p></p>
