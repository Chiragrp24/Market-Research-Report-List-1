<p><p>Vacuum chambers are essential in the semiconductor industry, specifically in the PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) and Etch processes. PVD involves depositing a thin layer of material onto a semiconductor wafer using a vacuum chamber to create a controlled environment for the deposition process. On the other hand, Etching is the process of removing specific layers from the semiconductor wafer, also done within a vacuum chamber. These chambers ensure that the deposition and etching processes occur in a contamination-free environment, resulting in high-quality semiconductor components.</p></p>
