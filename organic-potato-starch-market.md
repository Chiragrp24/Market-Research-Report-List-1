<p><p>Organic potato starch is available in two market types: native and modified. Native potato starch is extracted directly from potatoes and is the purest form of the starch. It is commonly used as a thickening agent in food products and has good water holding properties. Modified potato starch, on the other hand, goes through a process that alters its properties and makes it more versatile. Modified starch can withstand higher temperatures, has improved stability, and can be used in a wider range of applications, including as a binding agent, stabilizer, or even as a fat replacer.</p></p>
