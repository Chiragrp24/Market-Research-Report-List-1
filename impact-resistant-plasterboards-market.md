Tapered-Edged plasterboards have a slight bevel on the long edges, making it easier for joint compound to be applied and leveled. This type is commonly used for tape and jointing systems. Square-Edged plasterboards, on the other hand, have sharp edges and are typically used for traditional skim plastering. The choice between these two types depends on the specific application and desired finish.</p></p>
