<p><p>A vapor pressure osmometer is a laboratory instrument used to measure the osmotic pressure of a solution. It works on the principle of Raoult's Law, which states that the vapor pressure of a volatile solute in a solution is proportional to its mole fraction. By measuring the vapor pressure, the osmotic pressure can be determined, which is related to the concentration of solute particles in the solution.</p><p>The current outlook of the vapor pressure osmometer market is positive, and it is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13% during the forecasted period.
