<p><p>The intra-aortic balloon counterpulsation device is a mechanical device used to assist the heart in improving its function and blood flow. It is commonly used in patients with severe heart failure or those undergoing high-risk cardiac procedures. The device consists of a long catheter with an inflatable balloon at its tip, which is inserted into the aorta, the main artery that carries oxygenated blood from the heart to the rest of the body.</p><p>The balloon inflates during diastole, or the resting phase of the heart, to increase coronary blood flow and oxygen supply to the heart muscle. It deflates during systole, or the contracting phase, reducing the afterload on the heart and improving its efficiency. This counterpulsation effect helps to enhance cardiac output and decrease the workload of the heart.</p><p>According to market analysis, the intra-aortic balloon counterpulsation device market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 7.7% during the forecast period.
