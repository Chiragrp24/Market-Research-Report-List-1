<p><p>Cassava pulp is a byproduct obtained from the processing of cassava roots, a staple crop widely grown in tropical regions. It is mainly composed of cellulose, hemicellulose, and lignin, and is commonly utilized as an animal feed ingredient due to its abundance and low cost. Additionally, cassava pulp can be processed into bioethanol, which is a renewable and sustainable source of energy.</p><p>The future outlook of the cassava pulp market appears promising, driven by several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for animal feed ingredients, particularly in developing countries, is expected to fuel the demand for cassava pulp. It offers a cost-effective alternative to conventional feed ingredients, such as corn and soybean meal. Moreover, the rising popularity of bioethanol as a clean and renewable energy source is anticipated to drive the demand for cassava pulp in the biofuel industry.</p><p>Furthermore, cassava pulp serves as a potential source of valuable chemical compounds, such as antioxidants and fibers, which can find applications in the food and pharmaceutical industries. The increasing awareness regarding the health benefits of these compounds is likely to contribute to market growth.</p><p>However, certain challenges may hinder the growth of the cassava pulp market. Limited research and development efforts aimed at improving the processing and utilization of cassava pulp may restrict its potential applications. Ongoing efforts to enhance its value-added applications and capitalize on its nutritional benefits are expected to contribute to its market growth.</p></p>

It is commonly used in animal feed due to its high nutritional content. Wet cassava pulp, on the other hand, is the byproduct of cassava processing and is often used to produce alcohol or converted into biogas.
