<p><p>Access Control is a security measure that restrains or permits entry to a physical or digital space. There are various types of access controls, two of them being card-based and biometrics. Card-based access controls use smartcards or proximity cards to grant access, requiring the user to swipe or tap their card. On the other hand, biometric access controls employ unique physiological or behavioral characteristics like fingerprints or face recognition to verify and grant access. Both methods offer enhanced security by ensuring only authorized individuals can enter restricted areas or systems.</p></p>
