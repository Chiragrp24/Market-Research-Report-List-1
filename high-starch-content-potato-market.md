<p><p>The high starch content potato market is divided into two main types: conventional and micro propagation. Conventional potatoes are produced through the natural process of planting tubers in the soil, yielding a variety of sizes and shapes. On the other hand, micro propagation involves using tissue culture techniques to produce uniform and disease-free potato plants. This method allows for the production of large quantities of identical potatoes, ensuring consistency in size, shape, and quality. Both market types cater to different consumer preferences and have their own advantages and disadvantages.</p></p>
