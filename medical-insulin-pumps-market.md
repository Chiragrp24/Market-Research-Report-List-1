<p><p>Medical insulin pumps are devices used by individuals with diabetes to deliver insulin. There are two types of insulin pumps in the market: patch pump and normal pump. A patch pump is a small, adhesive device that is attached to the body and delivers insulin through a small needle. It provides a discreet and convenient option for insulin delivery. On the other hand, a normal pump is a larger device that is worn externally, typically clipped onto clothing or a belt. It also delivers insulin through a small tube or cannula. Both types of pumps help individuals manage their insulin levels effectively.</p></p>
