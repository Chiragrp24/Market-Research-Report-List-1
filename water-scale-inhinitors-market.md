<p><p>Water scale inhibitors are used to prevent the accumulation of mineral deposits, or scale, in water systems. They can be classified into two main types: inorganic and organic. Inorganic scale inhibitors, such as polyphosphates, silicates, and phosphonates, work by forming a soluble complex with calcium and other mineral ions, preventing their precipitation. Organic scale inhibitors, including polymers and surfactants, function by adsorbing onto the surface of mineral crystals and inhibiting their growth. Both types of inhibitors are widely used in various industries and residential applications to maintain the efficiency and prolong the lifespan of water systems.</p></p>
