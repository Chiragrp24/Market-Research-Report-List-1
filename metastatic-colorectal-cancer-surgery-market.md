<p><p>Metastatic colorectal cancer surgery market includes different types of procedures. Tumor resection involves removing the tumor and surrounding tissue from the colon or rectum. This surgical approach aims to eliminate the cancer cells and prevent further spreading. On the other hand, portal vein embolization is a preoperative procedure in which the blood supply to a part of the liver is blocked, encouraging the growth of healthy liver tissue. This technique is used to prepare patients with liver metastases for surgery by improving liver function and reducing the risk of complications.</p></p>
