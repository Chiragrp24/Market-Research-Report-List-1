<p><p>Automotive speed radar is a device that uses radio waves to determine the speed of vehicles. It is commonly used by law enforcement agencies for speed enforcement and can also be found in some car models for advanced driver assistance systems. The speed radar calculates the speed of the target vehicle by analyzing the frequency shift of the radio waves reflected off it.</p><p>The automotive speed radar market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. There are several factors driving this growth, such as rising concerns about road safety and the increasing adoption of advanced driver assistance systems. The demand for automotive speed radars is also fueled by the need for improved traffic management systems and the enforcement of speed limits.</p><p>In terms of market analysis, the global automotive speed radar market is segmented based on technology, range, and application. The technology segment includes radar-based and LIDAR-based speed radars. The radar-based segment holds a significant market share due to its lower cost and better resistance to weather conditions. However, the LIDAR-based segment is expected to witness rapid growth in the forecast period due to its higher accuracy.</p><p>Based on range, the market is divided into long-range, medium-range, and short-range speed radars. The long-range segment is projected to dominate the market due to its ability to detect speeding vehicles from a distance. However, the medium-range segment is anticipated to grow at the highest CAGR during the forecast period.</p><p>Regionally, North America is expected to be the largest market for automotive speed radars due to strict regulations and high adoption of advanced driver assistance systems.
