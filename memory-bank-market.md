<p><p>Memory Bank market types refer to the various forms in which computer memory is packaged and sold. Dual In-Line Memory Modules (DIMM) are the current industry standard, consisting of multiple memory chips on a small circuit board that plugs into a computer's motherboard. They offer higher capacity and improved performance. Single In-Line Memory Modules (SIMM) were widely used in older computers and had memory chips on one side of the circuit board. However, they had limitations on capacity and slower speeds compared to DIMMs.</p></p>
