<p><p>Porous glass foam, also known as cellular or spongy glass, is a lightweight, highly porous material with a wide range of applications. It is created by heating powdered or crushed glass mixed with a foaming agent that promotes bubble formation. As the glass melts and fuses, the foaming agent decomposes, causing the formation of gas-filled bubbles. The resulting material has a three-dimensional network of interconnected pores. </p><p>The porous structure of glass foam provides excellent properties such as low thermal conductivity, high compressive strength, and good acoustic insulation. These characteristics make it suitable for various applications such as thermal insulation, soundproofing, filtration, burners, sensors, and lightweight composites.</p><p>The global porous glass foam market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. <p><p>The porous glass foam market can be classified into three types: black (gray) foam glass, white foam glass, and others (multicolor). Black foam glass is composed of recycled glass with carbon added, giving it a dark gray color. White foam glass, on the other hand, is made purely from silica-based glass, resulting in a white appearance. The "others" category encompasses foam glass products with multicolor variations, possibly achieved through the addition of pigments or other materials. These different types offer diverse options for consumers in the porous glass foam market.</p></p>
