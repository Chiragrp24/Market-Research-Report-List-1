<p><p>Fire penetration seals are used to prevent the spread of fire through openings, such as pipes or cables, in buildings. These seals come in different types, including foam, block, ablative coating, and others. Foam seals are made of fire-resistant materials and expand to fill gaps during a fire. Block seals are solid barriers that withstand high temperatures. Ablative coatings create a protective layer when exposed to heat. Other types may include intumescent mastics, putty, or wraps. These seals help maintain the fire compartmentalization, limiting the fire's ability to spread through penetrations.</p></p>
